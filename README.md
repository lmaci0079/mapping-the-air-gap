# Mapping-The-Air-Gap
Spatial Analysis of PM2.5 and Respiratory Risk Across Chicago Neighborhoods

# 🌬️ Chicago Air Quality & Respiratory Risk: A Spatial Analysis
This project examines whether all Chicago communities experience the same air quality by analyzing spatial patterns of PM2.5 pollution and their relationship to respiratory health risk. This work brings together environmental, demographic, and health‑related datasets to explore how pollution exposure varies across neighborhoods and whether those variations align with socioeconomic and racial inequities.

## 🗺️ Methods & Workflow
Data acquisition from environmental, demographic, and health sources
Data cleaning and transformation in R
Spatial visualization of PM2.5 and respiratory risk across Chicago
Equity analysis comparing pollution burden with socioeconomic and racial indicators

## 📊 Key Questions
Which Chicago neighborhoods experience the highest PM2.5 levels?
How does respiratory vulnerability vary across the city?
Do pollution hotspots overlap with disadvantaged communities?

## 🔧 Tools Used
R and RStudio
tidyverse, sf, ggplot2, tmap
R Markdown for reproducible reporting
